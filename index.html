Jon Snow's Successes

  • Bastard of Winterfell (Book 1 - "A Game of Thrones"): Jon Snow begins his journey as the illegitimate son of Eddard Stark, Lord of Winterfell.
  • Joining the Night's Watch (Book 1 - "A Game of Thrones"): Jon chooses to join the Night's Watch, seeking a life of honor and purpose.
  • Friendship with Samwell Tarly (Book 1 - "A Game of Thrones"): Jon forms a close friendship with Samwell Tarly and helps him integrate into the Night's Watch.
  • Defending Castle Black (Book 3 - "A Storm of Swords"): Jon plays a vital role in the defense of Castle Black during the wildlings' attack, earning respect and leadership among his fellow members.
  • Becoming Lord Commander (Book 3 - "A Storm of Swords"): After the death of Lord Commander Jeor Mormont, Jon is elected as the new Lord Commander of the Night's Watch.
  • Negotiating with Stannis Baratheon (Book 5 - "A Dance with Dragons"): Jon negotiates with Stannis Baratheon and secures his aid in defending the Wall against the wildlings.
  • Forming Alliances Beyond the Wall (Book 5 - "A Dance with Dragons"): Jon builds relationships with various wildling leaders, including Tormund Giantsbane, as he seeks to integrate them into the realm to face the threat of the Others (White Walkers).
  • Confronting the Others (Book 5 - "A Dance with Dragons"): Jon takes the initiative to confront the threat beyond the Wall and ventures into dangerous territory to learn more about the Others and their capabilities.
  • Death and Resurrection (Book 5 - "A Dance with Dragons"): Jon is betrayed and stabbed by his own men at Castle Black. His fate is left uncertain, but there are strong hints that he might be resurrected.
